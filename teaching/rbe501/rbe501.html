RBE501: Robot Dynamics



Welcome to Robot Dynamics! This course is focused on the foundations and principles of dynamic analysis of serial arm manipulators. Topics, such as dynamic modeling of robotic arms using Newton and Lagrange techniques will be covered together with advanced techniques for kinematic analysis.

What is this course about?


This course covers the foundations and principles of robot dynamics. Topics include system modeling including dynamical modeling of serial arm robots using Newton and Lagrange’s techniques, dynamical modeling of mobile robots, introduction to dynamics-based robot control, as well as advanced techniques for serial arm forward kinematics, trajectory planning, singularity and manipulability, and vision-based control. In addition, dynamic simulation techniques will be covered to apply the concepts learned using realistic simulators. An end of term team project would allow students to apply mastery of the subject to real-world robotic platforms. (Prerequisite: RBE 500 or equivalent.

Course Outcomes (COs)


  • Mathematically derive dynamic model of serial manipulators.
  • Perform dynamic analysis of serial manipulators.
  • Solve for forward kinematics of serial manipulators using advanced approaches.
  • Perform singularity analysis of serial robots.
  • Perform trajectory planning of serial robots.
  • Understand dynamics-based control of serial robots.
  • Understand motion analysis of mobile robots.
  • Simulate serial robotic platforms.
  • Report on serial robotics literature – state of the art.
  • Apply concepts to a comprehensive hands-on project.


Prerequisite


RBE 500 or equivalent.
